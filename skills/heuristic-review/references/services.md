# Services reference

Ollie's current service offerings, and how they map to the kinds of problems a heuristic review turns up. Edit this file directly to update the list, add/remove services, or tweak the mapping — the skill re-reads it every run, no need to touch `SKILL.md` itself.

## Services

Grouped by type, for the report's "Full list of services" section. Each service has a plain-English description covering what it entails and why it's useful — written for a client who isn't a UX professional, so avoid jargon here even where the service *name* itself is a bit of an industry term. The skill renders these groups and descriptions directly into the report, so keep the wording client-ready, not shorthand notes to self.

### Audits

Focused reviews that find and prioritise specific problems, without redesigning anything.

- **Conversion funnel audit (checkout, signup, booking, trial)** — I go through your checkout, signup, booking or trial flow step by step and flag exactly where people are likely dropping off, and why. Useful if you can see visitors starting a flow but not finishing it.
- **Accessibility audit (WCAG spot-check)** — A check against the internationally recognised accessibility standard (WCAG), covering things like colour contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen-reader support. Useful for reducing legal risk and making sure you're not accidentally locking out disabled users.
- **Information architecture review** — A look at how your content and navigation are organised, to check people can actually find what they're looking for. Useful if users often seem lost or end up in the wrong section.
- **Landing page conversion audit** — A focused review of one specific marketing or landing page against what's stopping visitors from taking the action you want (signing up, buying, enquiring). Useful before running paid traffic to a page.
- **Design system consistency audit** — A check for where buttons, colours, spacing or components are used inconsistently across your product. Useful for keeping a growing product looking coherent and professional as more people build on it.

### Research

Understanding your actual users, rather than guessing on their behalf.

- **Usability testing (moderated / unmoderated + findings report)** — Watching real people try to complete real tasks on your product, either live with me guiding them (moderated) or recorded independently (unmoderated), followed by a written report of what went wrong and why. The most direct way to find out how people actually use your product, rather than how you assume they do.
- **Competitive UX benchmarking (you vs. 2–3 competitors)** — A side-by-side comparison of how your product handles a specific task compared to 2–3 competitors. Useful for knowing where you're genuinely behind (or ahead) before deciding what to prioritise.
- **Card sort / IA workshop** — A structured exercise where real users group and label your content the way it makes sense to them, revealing how they naturally think about your product. Useful for reorganising a confusing menu or site structure from the ground up.
- **Persona / user journey mapping session** — A collaborative session that pins down who your users actually are and maps out the full journey they take with your product, highlighting where the experience currently breaks down. Useful as a shared reference point for a team making product decisions.

### Writing & content

- **Microcopy / UX writing pass (errors, empty states, CTAs)** — Rewriting the small pieces of text that guide people through your product — error messages, empty states, button labels — so they're clear and helpful rather than confusing or robotic. Useful because small wording problems quietly cause real drop-off.

### Design & fixes

Building the solution, not just diagnosing the problem.

- **Form design audit or redesign** — Reviewing (or rebuilding) a specific form — its field order, labelling, validation and error handling — so people can complete it with less friction and fewer mistakes. Useful for any form where people are abandoning partway through or getting stuck on errors.
- **Onboarding flow audit or redesign** — Reviewing (or rebuilding) what a brand-new user experiences in their first session, so they understand your product quickly enough to stick around. Useful if new users sign up but don't come back.
- **Wireframe or prototype for one specific flow fix** — A concrete, ready-to-build design for one specific flow that's not working, rather than just a diagnosis of what's wrong. Useful when you already know a problem exists and want a solution in hand, not just a findings report.

## Problem → service mapping

Use this when writing the closing "how I can help" section. It's many-to-many — a single finding can point at more than one service, and a service can resolve findings from more than one focus lens. Pick whichever service(s) most directly resolve the specific finding, don't force every finding into a slot.

| Kind of problem found | Likely service(s) |
|---|---|
| Friction in checkout, signup, booking, or trial flows | Conversion funnel audit |
| General task-completion confusion, unclear next steps | Usability testing |
| Contrast, focus states, screen-reader/semantic issues, keyboard access | Accessibility audit (WCAG spot-check) |
| Confusing navigation, unclear site structure, users can't find things | Information architecture review; Card sort / IA workshop |
| Form friction — validation, labelling, error handling, field order | Form design audit or redesign |
| Jargon, unclear labels, unhelpful error messages, weak CTAs | Microcopy / UX writing pass |
| New users confused in their first session, unclear first-run experience | Onboarding flow audit or redesign |
| A landing/marketing page isn't converting visitors | Landing page conversion audit |
| Inconsistent components, spacing, or patterns across pages | Design system consistency audit |
| One specific flow is broken and needs a concrete fix, not just a diagnosis | Wireframe or prototype for one specific flow fix |
| Uncertainty about who the users actually are or how they think | Persona / user journey mapping session |
| Client wants to know how they compare to rivals | Competitive UX benchmarking |

## No direct match

Some findings (e.g. performance-adjacent observations that come up incidentally) won't map cleanly to any service above. Don't force a mapping — report the finding on its own merits and let the closing section's general "not sure which of these fits, let's talk" contact prompt cover it.
