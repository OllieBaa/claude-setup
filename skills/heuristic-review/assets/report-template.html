What is a heuristic review?

A heuristic review is a structured expert evaluation of a product's usability, measured against ten well-established principles of good interface design (Jakob Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics). Rather than testing with real users, independent reviewers each examine the product and flag anywhere it works against those principles — then compare notes to separate the issues that matter most from the ones that are easy for any one person to miss.

This report used five independent reviewers, each looking at the product through a different lens: accessibility, usability & flow, legibility & readability, colour & visual contrast, and content clarity. Where more than one reviewer independently flagged the same issue, that's noted below — it's a strong signal the problem is real and worth prioritising.

Heuristic reviews are fast, low-cost, and catch a large share of usability problems before you invest in full user testing or a redesign. They're not a replacement for testing with real users, but they're an efficient first pass that shows you where to focus.

Methodology: Nielsen Norman Group, "How to Conduct a Heuristic Evaluation" · Heuristics: "10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design"
